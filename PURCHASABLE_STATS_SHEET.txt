UNIT ANALYSIS & BALANCE NOTES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

KEY MISSION MECHANIC (Essential Context):
  • Mission reward formula: baseSpheres × (1 + carry * 0.01)
  • Mission duration formula: baseDays / (speedMultiplier)
  • Scout: 500-1000 S base | 1 day base duration | 2% casualty rate
  • Attack: 500-1500 S base | 1.5 day base duration | 10% casualty rate | 15% Gemheart chance
  • Carry bonus: Each carry point = +1% mission reward
  • Speed bonus: Each speed point = +1% mission completion rate = More missions/day = More Gemhearts/day

✓ STRATEGIC UNITS (Well-Designed):

  1. BRIDGECREWS (5 S, +0.01 speed per unit):
     - ROLE: Speed farming for Gemhearts
     - MECHANIC: Each 100 Bridgecrews = 1.0 speed bonus = missions complete 2x faster
     - ECONOMY: 100 Bridgecrews cost only 500 S to recruit
     - STRATEGIC VALUE: With 100 Bridgecrews, attack missions finish in 45 mins (dev time) instead of 
       90 mins = 2x more missions/day = 2x faster Gemheart farming
     - SYNERGY: Add Gravity Lift fabrial (5 GH, 2x speed) = massive speedup
     - FARMER LOADOUT: 100 Bridgecrews + 10 Spearmen + 10 Chulls = fast, profitable attacks
     - RECOMMENDATION: ✓ WELL-BALANCED. Central to Gemheart economy.
  
  2. CHULLS (50 S, 10 carry per unit):
     - ROLE: Mission profit multiplier
     - MECHANIC: 10 carry = +10% mission rewards across ALL mission types
     - ECONOMY: Cost 50 S per Chull, adds 50-150 S per attack mission (pay for itself in 1-3 attacks)
     - EXAMPLE: Scout without carry: 500-1000 S. Scout with 1 Chull: 550-1100 S. (+50-100 S gain)
     - SYNERGY: More carry = more profit per mission = better sphere income
     - FARMER LOADOUT: Same 100 Bridgecrews + 10 Spearmen + 10 Chulls gives +10% loot on every attack
     - SPEED PENALTY: -0.025 per Chull is small (100 Chulls = -2.5 speed) and worth the carry
     - RECOMMENDATION: ✓ WELL-BALANCED. Efficient profit multiplier for farming playstyle.
  
  3. SPEARMEN (10 S, 1.0 power):
     - ROLE: Efficient base unit for farming missions
     - MECHANIC: Cheapest power source (10 S per power point)
     - MISSION CONTEXT: Attacks need ~50 power to beat enemy (50-100 range). 50 Spearmen = 500 S investment
     - ECONOMY: Lowest cost-to-power = default farming unit
     - RECOMMENDATION: ✓ WELL-BALANCED. Backbone of farming armies.

⚠ QUESTIONABLE BALANCE:

  1. ARCHERS (15 S, 1.0 power, 90% survival):
     - ISSUE: 50% more expensive than Spearmen for 15% better survival
     - MISSION CONTEXT: In mission economy where power > enemy suffices, survival doesn't matter
       (Attacks: player power vs ~50-100 enemy, Spearmen 75% survival still fine)
     - STRATEGIC WEAKNESS: No unique role. Just "expensive Spearmen"
     - COMPARISON: 100 Archers (1500 S) vs 100 Spearmen (1000 S) = +5% survival for +500 S cost
     - That 500 S buys: 100 Bridgecrews (2x speed!) OR 10 Chulls (+10% profit!)
     - RECOMMENDATION: CONSIDER reducing to 12 S (40% markup instead of 50%) OR give unique bonus
       (e.g., "vs armored foes" bonus in future PvP system)
     - CURRENT STATUS: Underpowered compared to strategic alternatives

  2. SHARDBEARERS (1 Gemheart, 2.0x power multiplier per unit):
     - MECHANIC: Each Shardbearer multiplies TOTAL army power by 2.0
     - EXAMPLE: 50 Spearmen = 50 power. Add 1 Shardbearer = (50 + 0) * 2.0 = 100 power
     - EXAMPLE: 50 Spearmen + 2 Shardbearers = 50 * 3.0 = 150 power (multiplicative stacking)
     - COST: 1 Gemheart per Shardbearer = EXTREME gating (Gemhearts are scarcest resource)
     - STRATEGIC VALUE: "Late-game power spike" / "Gemheart sink"
     - ECONOMY QUESTION: Is 2.0x worth 1 GH? Let's compare:
       • 1 GH = 10,000 S at black market exchange
       • Alternative: Buy 10,000 S of Spearmen (1000 Spearmen) = 1000 power instead of 2x multiplier
       • 1 Shardbearer 2x multiplier only breaks even vs raw Spearmen if you have 5000+ power already
     - RECOMMENDATION: WELL-DESIGNED. Shardbearers are correctly rare/expensive. They're for endgame
       power multiplication when you already have large armies.

✗ UNDERPOWERED UNITS:

  1. NOBLES (50 S, 50% survival):
     - ISSUE: 50% survival is lowest in entire game
     - COMPARISON: Same cost as Chull (50 S) but Chull has 80% survival + 10 carry bonus
     - PROBLEM: For espionage missions, 50% survival means constant agent casualties
     - RECOMMENDATION: Increase to 65% survival OR reduce cost to 30 S

ESPIONAGE UNITS (Separate economy - checked vs espionage mission mechanics):
  • Spy Agent (150 S): Reasonable middle-tier option for espionage
  • Ghostblood (600 S): Premium espionage unit, 90% survival justified for cost tier

UNIT BALANCE SUMMARY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
✓ Well-designed & Strategic: Bridgecrews (speed farming), Chulls (profit multiplier), Spearmen (base unit)
✓ Correctly Gated: Shardbearers (expensive for endgame power, appropriately Gemheart-locked)
⚠ Needs Minor Adjustment: Archers (underpowered vs alternatives, no unique role)
✗ Underpowered: Nobles (50% survival too low for cost)

MISSION FARMING OPTIMIZATION (Buildings that multiply mission efficiency):
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

⚠ TRAINING CAMP (7,000 S, +10% power):
  - COST: Extremely high (28x a Market)
  - EFFECT: +10% to all unit power (stacking: 2 camps = +20%, etc.)
  - MISSION CONTEXT: Energy spent on +10% power could instead buy:
    • 280 Spearmen (1400 S remaining)

  - PROBLEM: +10% power is passive/invisible vs buying 280 Spearmen (+280 power)
  - WHERE IT MATTERS: Late-game when you already have 1000+ power armies
    (where +10% = +100 power worth having)
  - EARLY GAME: Terrible investment (better to buy units)
  - RECOMMENDATION: BALANCE IS OK for endgame, but feels "trap" for early players.

⚠ MONASTERY (7,000 S, +5% survival, 50 S/day upkeep):
  - COST: Same as Training Camp (extremely high)
  - EFFECT: +5% to all unit survival rate (stacking multiplier)

    (Just need power > enemy. Spearmen 75% → +5% = 80% doesn't meaningfully change outcomes)
  - UPKEEP: 50 S/day ongoing = 18,250 S lifetime cost (to break even takes forever)
  - WHERE IT MATTERS: PvP combat where every % helps OR espionage where casualties matter
  - EARLY GAME: Terrible investment (no measurable benefit)
  - RECOMMENDATION: UNDERPOWERED. Either:
    a) Reduce cost to 3,500 S (half current cost)
    b) Increase bonus to +10% survival (2x current)
    c) Reduce upkeep to 25 S/day or make it free
    Current design most valuable to experienced players doing PvP.

FABRIAL ANALYSIS & BALANCE NOTES:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CONTEXTUAL METAGAME:
  Two farming strategies emerge:
  1. SPEED FARM: Bridgecrews (speed bonus) + Gravity Lift (2x speed) = 4-6x mission speed
     → Complete missions in 15-45 mins instead of 90 mins = 4x more missions/day = 4x Gemhearts
  2. PROFIT FARM: Chulls (carry) + Ledger (2x income) = +20% mission rewards
     → More spheres per mission = more resources for next mission

MISSION FABRIALS - STRATEGIC VALUE:
───────────────────────────────────

✓ GRAVITY LIFT (5 GH, 2.0x mission speed):
  - MECHANIC: Cuts mission duration in half (or to 1/3 with boost)
  - STRATEGIC IMPACT: Massive efficiency multiplier for farming
  - EXAMPLE: Normal attack mission takes 1.5 days. With Gravity Lift: 45 minutes (dev time)
    = 32 missions/day instead of 16 missions/day = 2x Gemheart generation speed
  - SYNERGY: Stacks with Bridgecrews (can go 4-6x speed combo!)
  - BUILD ORDER: Research Library → Gravity Lift = TOP PRIORITY (enables speed farming)
  - COST vs VALUE: 5 GH is expensive, but enables entire speed-farm playstyle
  - RECOMMENDATION: ✓ WELL-BALANCED. Mission speed multiplier is powerful enough to justify cost.

✗ LEDGER (3 GH, 1.5x market income):
  - MECHANIC: All Markets produce +50% income (400 → 600 S/day per Market)
  - PROBLEM: Income from Markets is passive/indirect vs Gemheart sources
  - ROI ANALYSIS: 3 GH = 30,000 S equivalent at exchange rate
    • With 1 Market: +200 S/day income = 150 days to break even (!)
    • With 5 Markets: +1000 S/day income = 30 days to break even
    • With 10 Markets: +2000 S/day income = 15 days to break even
  - CONTEXT: Early game players won't have 10 markets for a while
  - WHEN VALUABLE: Late-game when you have 10+ markets (generates perpetual income)
  - EARLY GAME: Terrible investment (break-even takes too long)
  - COMPARISON: Spend 3 GH on Gravity Lift (2x speed farm = 2x more missions = more direct GH gain)
    vs Ledger (passive income multiplier with slow ROI)
  - RECOMMENDATION: ⚠ SITUATIONAL. Re-evaluate cost:
    a) Reduce to 1-2 GH (makes early-game viable), OR
    b) Increase to 2.0-2.5x multiplier (faster ROI on investment)
    Current: Best for late-game wealthy players, terrible for progression players

⚠ HEATRIAL (3 GH, 1.5x provision cap):
  - MECHANIC: Multiply all provision cap by 1.5x
    • Without Heatrial: 150 base + (50 per Soulcaster)
    • With Heatrial: (150 base + (50 per Soulcaster)) × 1.5
    • Example: Base 150 + 5 Soulcasters (250) = 400 total, then × 1.5 = 600 provisions
  - STRATEGIC VALUE: Larger provision pool = bigger armies = more mission power
  - COMPARISON TO LEDGER: Same cost (3 GH) but different value type
    • Ledger: +50% passive income (long-term)
    • Heatrial: More army cap NOW (direct power increase)
  - SYNERGY: More provisions = can run bigger armed missions = more rewards
  - WHEN VALUABLE: When you're provision-capped and want bigger armies
  - RECOMMENDATION: ✓ REASONABLE. Direct army expansion vs passive income makes it more useful than Ledger.

SECTION 4: RESOURCE ECONOMY BALANCE REVIEW
================================================================================

RESOURCE FLOW RATES (Dev Mode - 10 second days):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Starting Resources:
  • Spheres: 25,000 S (dev)
  • Gemhearts: 10 GH (dev)
  • Provisions: 150
  • Territory: 0/50

Daily Income Sources:
  • Base market income: 0 S/day (must build Market = +400 S/day each)
  • Gemheart sources: 10% of attack raids ONLY, plateau runs (coalition), exchange 10 GH = 10,000 S
  • Provision sources: +50 per Soulcaster Bunker (expires with death/mission)

GEMHEART SCARCITY ANALYSIS:
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Problem: Gemhearts are 10x scarcer than Spheres
  - Only 3 obtainable ways: Raid drops (10% chance), Plateau runs, Market exchange (10,000 S)
  - All fabrials require 1-5 Gemhearts
  - All high-tier spy units require Gemhearts (Shardbearers = 1 GH each)
  - All arena fabrials require Gemhearts

Implication: Players will face "GH bottleneck" around mid-game
  - Can't afford everything that costs Gemhearts
  - Must choose between: fabrials vs. Shardbearers vs. arena equipment

Recommendation: CHECK that this is intentional gating design.
  If you want Gemhearts scarce for challenge: STATUS QUO is good.
  If you want Gemhearts more accessible: Consider increasing raid drop to 15% or
  adding more plateau run frequency.

SPHERE ECONOMY ANALYSIS:
────────────────────────
Income sources (Dev mode testing):
  • Market: 400 S/day per Market (easy to scale)
  • Scout mission: 500-1,500 S (1 day mission)
  • Attack mission: 2,000-5,000 S (1.5 day mission)
  • Conquest mission: Returns cost only, uses 200 S + 100 provision as cost
  • Plateau run: Shared loot, variable (2 day mission)

Spending sinks:
  • Unit recruitment: 5-600 S per unit (scaling)
  • Building construction: 150-10,000 S (one-time per type)
  • Upkeep: 25-75 S/day per building
  • Conquest cost: 200 S + 100 provision per conquest attempt

Assessment: Sphere income feels abundant for mid-game (Markets scale well)
  Recommendation: BALANCED. Players should have constant sphere income to spend.


PROVISION ECONOMY ANALYSIS:
────────────────────────────
Starting: 150 provisions
  • Each unit costs 1-3 provisions per recruitment (one-time)
  • Army size determines provision "cap" (200-unit cap=200 provisions used)
  • Soulcasters increase cap: +50 per bunker
  • Heatrial multiplier: 1.5x cap total

Problem: Unclear if provisions gate army size or just add flavor cost
  - If provisions are HARD CAP on army: This matters a lot (balance critical)
  - If provisions are SOFT mechanic: Less critical but still important

Recommendation: CLARIFY in UI whether provisions are:
  • Hard cap (can't recruit more units if at provision limit)
  • Soft penalty (upkeep cost increase if over cap)
  Either way, current design works but needs clearer communication.


TERRITORY ECONOMY ANALYSIS:
───────────────────────────
Starting: 0/50 territory
  • Each conquest: +10-25 territory per success
  • Land cost: Buildings require 1-5 land each
  • Max capacity: 50 territory

Problem: Land seems abundant (50 total allows ~8-10 buildings)
  - Most players won't hit territory cap
  - Conquest feels like "endgame expansion" not "early-game bottleneck"

Recommendation: Either:
  • Increase building land costs (make land more contested)
  • Decrease territory rewards per conquest (make expansion slower)
  • Increase territory cap (if abundance is intentional)
  As-is: Territory cap is not a binding constraint for most players.

SECTION 5: OVERALL BALANCE RECOMMENDATIONS (Priority Order)
================================================================================

SYSTEM ASSESSMENT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
After careful analysis of mission mechanics and the carry/speed economy:

THE GAME DESIGN IS WELL-CONCEIVED. There are emergent farming strategies:
  • Speed Farm: Bridgecrews + Gravity Lift = 4-6x mission frequency = fast Gemheart generation
  • Profit Farm: Chulls + Ledger = +20% mission rewards = sphere efficiency
  • Power Farm: Shardbearers + Training Camp = raw military scaling = conquest dominance

These strategies require different unit/building/fabrial combinations, creating
meaningful economic decisions. The design WORKS once you understand carry/speed mechanics.

WHAT NEEDS ADJUSTMENT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PHASE 1 - PRIORITY BALANCE TWEAKS (Low effort, clarify player experience):
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. [BALANCE] ARCHERS (15 S) - GIVE UNIQUE ROLE
   Current: Boring (just more-expensive Spearmen)
   Problem: No reason to buy these vs Spearmen for farming
   Recommendation: Add unique mechanic, e.g.:
     a) Bonus damage vs armored enemies (futuristic content)
     b) Ranged mechanic: "15% chance attacks defeat enemy with 0 casualties"
     c) Special mission: Archers get +2% Gemheart drop chance
   Benefit: Makes Archers a strategic choice, not just "expensive Spearmen"

2. [BALANCE] NOBLES (50 S) - INCREASE SURVIVAL
   Current: 50% survival (lowest in game, dies constantly)
   Problem: Too fragile for 50 S cost (Chulls are better value)
   Recommendation: Increase to 65-70% survival
   Benefit: Makes early espionage recruitment viable without constant losses

3. [CLARITY] ADD TOOLTIP EXPLANATIONS
   Current: Players don't understand carry bonus or speed bonus without reading code
   Recommendation: Add UI tooltips showing:
     • "Carry: +1% mission rewards per point"
     • "Speed: Faster mission completion (2 missions/day instead of 1)"
     • "Power: Army damage output vs enemy"
   Benefit: New players understand strategic value of Bridgecrews/Chulls immediately

PHASE 1.5 - IF ECONOMY FEELS OFF IN PLAYTESTING:
───────────────────────────────────────────────

4. [OPTIONAL] MONASTERY (7,000 S) - REDUCE COST OR INCREASE EFFECT
   Current: Feels like trap investment early game (+5% survival doesn't matter vs cheap units)
   Recommendation: Either:
     a) Reduce cost to 3,500 S (half), or
     b) Increase to +10% survival, or
     c) Add secondary effect (e.g., "units cost 5% less provisions")
   Only do if playtesters feel it's underpowered

5. [OPTIONAL] LEDGER (3 GH) - REDUCE COST FOR EARLY ACCESS
   Current: 150+ day ROI makes it late-game only
   Recommendation: Reduce to 1-2 GH (or increase mult to 2.0x)
   Only do if Ledger should be reachable for mid-game players

PHASE 2 - IF ARENA BALANCE IS NEEDED:
────────────────────────────────────

6. [TESTING] VALIDATE ARENA FABRIALS
   Current: Cost can't be evaluated without knowing arena damage/economy
   Action: Playtest 20+ arena duels with/without each fabrial
   Then: Analyze if pricing matches game impact

WHAT IS ACTUALLY WORKING WELL:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

✓ GAME DESIGN STRENGTHS (Keep these):

  • Bridgecrews strategic role: Speed farming is viable and interesting strategy
  • Chulls strategic role: Profit farming creates alternative playstyle
  • Shardbearers as Gemheart sink: Endgame power scaling with scarcity gates
  • Market economy: Perfect early-game income source, scalable forever
  • Mission reward formula: Carry bonus creates meaningful unit composition decisions
  • Gating systems: Spy Network/Research Library unlock systems without forcing them

✓ EMERGENT PLAYER STRATEGIES:

  New players will naturally discover (or fail to discover):
  1. Speed farmers: "Why are my missions so slow?" → Add Bridgecrews → "Much faster!"
  2. Profit farmers: "How do I get more sphere rewards?" → Add Chulls → "10% more loot!"
  3. Power farmers: "How do I crush enemies?" → Build Training Camps + recruit Shardbearers

This is GOOD game design - mechanics reward player experimentation.

  • Power requirement makes basic units valuable (don't need all Shardbearers)

✓ PROGRESSION GATING RESPECTS PLAYER PATHS:
  • Spy Network/Research Library unlock without forcing
  • Shardbearers are Gemheart sink for late-game power
  • Fabrials enable (but don't require) specific strategies

  • Spheres: Easy to earn, spent on units/buildings
  • Gemhearts: Scarce, spent on Shardbearers/Fabrials (creates meaningful choices)
